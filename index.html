About Me

Hi! I’m Reece, I’m a masters by research student at the University of Lincoln. Since tinkering with game engines all the way back in 2012, I’ve been hooked on building software and have experience working in teams, with clients and independently on various projects. Outside of building software, I spend my time playing drums (and dabble with guitar) in Lincoln’s worst named band, No Flamingos on a Sunday. We’ll play anything from Motorhead to Britney Spears, with some originals thrown in for good measure, though I like to think we sound better than the name does!

My master’s research is focused on gamifying musical-sight reading practice, for more information visit “RhythmTrain” in my portfolio. Work on this project has been peer reviewed and is being published in the International Conference on Computer Music later this year. If you’d like to see it in action, checkout the trailer below.

    Experience

    During my masters’ studies I’ve worked as an associate demonstrator to help undergraduate students and produced bite sized computer science tutorials for the schools YouTube channel. Outside of University, I’ve worked on various projects including as a programmer and project manager on the Halo fan game, Installation 01 (at various times between 2014-2019) and more recently as a freelance programmer with Strange Games on RC Airplane Challenge for Playstation 4 and Oculus VR headsets.
